Research

My research lies in development economics, with a focus on the well-being of women and children. My secondary fields are applied econometrics and economic demography.

Working Paper

  • Discounted Dreams: Aspirations and Time Preferences

    with Alexis Villacis and Will Hankins · R&R at the Journal of Agricultural and Resource Economics

    Using lab-in-the-field experiments with smallholder cocoa farmers in Ecuador, we examine how income aspirations relate to time preferences. We document a robust negative correlation between farmers' aspirations and the degree to which they discount future earnings, suggesting that interventions addressing aspirations and liquidity pressures could encourage longer-term investments.

    Second-Year Paper · August 2024 draft

    Examines the long-term effects of two Indonesian organizations—Dharma Wanita and PKK—on female labor market outcomes. Preliminary findings suggest that daughters of civil servants show a higher likelihood of formal-sector employment.
